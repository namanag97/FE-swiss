System Manifesto

// The Philosophy of Less

We adhere to the principles of Dieter Rams and the Bauhaus movement. An interface is not a canvas for decoration; it is a tool for understanding.

"Good design is as little design as possible. Less, but better — because it concentrates on the essential aspects."

Every pixel must justify its existence. Confusion is a design failure. The interface should disappear when it works.

01 · Clarity

Every element earns its place. Remove anything that does not communicate or function. Confusion is a design failure.

02 · Purity

No decoration for its own sake. Surface reflects structure. The interface disappears when it works as intended.

03 · Honesty

Components tell the truth about their state. Loading, empty, error — all visible, explicit, and never hidden from the user.
